Who is Kris Jenner?

Kris Jenner is a well-known American television personality, businesswoman, and manager who has played a significant role in shaping the careers and fortunes of her famous family, the Kardashians. Born on November 5, 1955, in San Diego, California, as Kristen Mary Houghton, Kris has become a household name in the world of entertainment and entrepreneurship.

Kris's journey to fame began when she married Robert Kardashian, a prominent attorney, in 1978. However, it was her later marriage to Olympic gold medalist Bruce Jenner, now known as Caitlyn Jenner, that truly thrust her into the public eye. The couple's marriage and family life became the focus of the reality television series "Keeping Up with the Kardashians," which premiered in 2007 and became a cultural phenomenon.

As the matriarch of the Kardashian-Jenner clan, Kris played a pivotal role in managing the careers of her children, including Kim, Kourtney, Khloé, and Rob Kardashian, as well as Kendall and Kylie Jenner. Under her guidance, the family has built a vast empire encompassing reality television shows, beauty and fashion brands, fragrances, and endorsement deals, among other ventures. Kris's business acumen and relentless work ethic have been instrumental in turning her family into multi-millionaires and household names.

Kris Jenner is often referred to as the "momager" due to her dual role as a mother and manager. Her shrewd negotiation skills and knack for turning controversies into opportunities have been central to the family's success. She has also been an advocate for open discussions about topics such as mental health, and raising awareness about issues that affect her family and society.

Beyond her involvement in the entertainment industry, Kris has ventured into various business endeavors, including co-founding the Kardashian Kollection fashion line and managing the careers of her children as they pursued their own entrepreneurial ventures.

Kris Jenner Career

Kris Jenner is an American media personality, socialite, and businesswoman. She is best known as the matriarch of the Kardashian-Jenner family and the creator and executive producer of the reality television series Keeping Up with the Kardashians (2007–2021).

Jenner began her career in the early 1980s as a flight attendant for American Airlines. After marrying Robert Kardashian in 1978, she became a stay-at-home mother to their four children: Kourtney, Kim, Khloé, and Rob. In 1991, she divorced Kardashian and married Olympian Caitlyn Jenner (formerly Bruce Jenner). The couple had two daughters together: Kendall and Kylie.

In the early 2000s, Jenner began managing her daughters' careers. She was instrumental in launching their clothing lines, fragrance empires, and other business ventures. In 2007, she pitched the idea for Keeping Up with the Kardashians to television producer Ryan Seacrest. The show became a huge success, and it launched Jenner and her family into international stardom.

Since the success of Keeping Up with the Kardashians, Jenner has expanded her business portfolio even further. She has launched her own production company, Jenner Communications, and she has executive produced several spin-off shows, including Kourtney and Khloe Take Miami, Kourtney and Kim Take New York, and Life of Kylie. She has also written a memoir, Kris Jenner... and All Things Kardashian (2011), which was a New York Times bestseller.
