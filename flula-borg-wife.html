How Did Flula Borg Become Famous?

Flula started making videos on ‘YouTube’ in 2007, showcasing his beatboxing and DJing skills. His videos about traveling in the USA gained popularity, and he quickly gathered over 700,000 subscribers within a couple of years. One of his videos, ‘Jennifer is a Party Pooper,’ went viral with 10 million views, bringing him widespread attention.

His online success led to appearances on TV shows like ‘The Today Show’ and ‘Last Call with Carson Daly.’ Flula continued his creative journey, hosting events like the ‘College Battle of the Bands’ and the ‘Bedrocktoberfest’ music festival. He gained recognition for his music, with hits like ‘Sweet Potato Casserole’ making waves.

In 2013, he joined the reality TV show ‘Auction Hunters’ and made his breakthrough in Hollywood with a role in ‘Pitch Perfect 2’ in 2015. Following this, he wrote, produced, and starred in the film ‘Buddymoon,’ earning acclaim at film festivals.

Flula expanded his acting career with roles in ‘Dirty 30’ (2016) and TV appearances on ‘Conan’ from 2016 to 2020. He ventured into voice acting for animated series and films like ‘The Boss Baby: Back in Business,’ ‘Tangled: The Series,’ and ‘Ralph Breaks the Internet.’

In 2021, he joined the ‘DC Cinematic Universe’ as the anti-superhero ‘Javelin’ in ‘Suicide Squad.’ Additionally, he launched his podcast ‘BoomTime’ in 2018. Flula continues to be active and diverse in his creative pursuits.
