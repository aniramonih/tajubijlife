NYPD rescues suicidal man who scaled the Brooklyn Bridge

A suicidal man climbed to the top of the Brooklyn Bridge Friday and was coaxed down after about three hours by cops who offered him cigarettes, the NYPD said.

The “despondent” made the climb before noon Friday and was “threatening to jump,” causing cops to stop traffic on the famed thoroughfare in both directions, NYPD officials said.

For three hours, the man remained atop the Manhattan-side tower on the south side of the bridge, facing the Statue of Liberty, with about a dozen officers trying to convince him to come down. He periodically walked to the edge, looked down, before sitting down again.

Lt. Michael Licitra from the NYPD’s Emergency Services Unit said officers used their negotiating skills to convince the man to voluntarily come down around 3 p.m.

“Like in the movies, this is like a negotiation, and there’s a give and take and we want something from him and he wants something from us. So we wanted him off the edge, and we were able to do that by giving him a cigarette,” Licitra told reporters later Friday following the daring rescue.
