Little Women: Atlanta stars break down in tears as they learn about Minnie Ross' car crash death in

THE cast of Little Women: Atlanta breaks down in tears in the show's season six trailer after learning of co-star Ashley "Minnie" Ross' car crash death.

The reality star passed away on April 27, 2020, at the age of 34.

The program's latest season returns on January 22 as Abira Greene, Shirlene "Ms. Juicy" King Pearson, "Tiny Twinz" Amanda and Andrea Salinas, Tiffany "Monie" Cashette, and Minnie showcase their ups and downs while in Atlanta.

While the season looks to kick off with a lot of partying - and some fights - the tune completely changes after the ladies learn of their friend's death.

In the clip, Monie asks Juicy: "Have you thought about how it's going to be without Minnie now?"

Viewers will also be able to see a memorial the stars throw for Minnie as they honor the late Lifetime star.

One scene shows Juicy trying not to breakdown as she says: "I'm not going to have nobody to argue with no more."

Abira also says: "Our friendship meant a lot to me because we started in the fire, but we walked through it together."

Originally it was believed that Minnie was a victim of a hit-and run, but The Sun exclusively revealed that her Nissan Sentra "hit the right side curb, lost control of the vehicle, crossed into the north bound lanes of travel and struck a Ford Focus head on that was traveling north bound.”

Minnie suffered “serious injuries” and was transported to an area hospital for treatment, where she succumbed to her injuries.

Plenty of reactions poured in after news of the tragic accident was revealed as Minnie's boyfriend wrote on Instagram: "The moment I saw you, I just knew it.

"I realized that you are all that I have been searching for all my life. I love you today, I love you tomorrow and I will love you the same 10 years from now.

"Ima miss you baby girl. RIP. Love of my life."

Minnie's mom, Tammie Jackson, also revealed that her daughter was able call her after the accident but "couldn’t speak."

At Minnie's funeral, Tamara said it gives her "comfort" knowing her daughter was thinking of her in the final moments of her life.

"May the angels sing to you in the most joyous way. Happy Birthday @msminnielwa!! You will forever remain in our hearts."
