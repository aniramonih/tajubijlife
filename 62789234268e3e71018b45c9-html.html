Boxing: Canelo Alvarez's disfigured face after painful loss to Dmitry Bivol

Saul "Canelo" Alvarez not only lost his second fight as a professional in his successful career, but it is also likely that he received last Saturday night in Las Vegas, Nevada, the worst beating of his career, at least that's what it seems after seeing his face as never before after a fight.

Since Round 4, Dmitry Bivol's punches took their toll on the Mexican boxer's physique, who began to redden in an unusual way and while the fight was going on, the native of Guadalajara, Jalisco could not keep his guard firm and that generated several lacerations that ended up being more than notorious at the end of the fight, which he lost by unanimous decision.

What did Canelo learn from the defeat against Bivol?

Many allude this defeat of Canelo to the decision to move up in weight and fight for the WBA (Super) light heavyweight title belt, due, above all, to the uncommon lack of energy that Alvarez showed especially in the final rounds of the main event.

After the result was known and despite the bad conditions he was in, Canelo took advantage of the microphones not only to recognize his rival and congratulate him for the victory, he also said "this can't stay like this", so he agreed to a future rematch.

Perhaps the blows received and even the defeat are not such bad news for the famous Mexican boxer, as they can serve as motivation for a new challenge in his career, to beat Bivol in a rematch and prove that he can learn from his own mistakes.

The words of Canelo Alvarez

"I felt I won, I did enough, but that's boxing, you win and you lose," Canelo said. "He's a good fighter, I felt his weight a lot, I couldn't connect him that easy. I made a great fight and in the end the people are the ones who win. There are no excuses, he is a great fighter, you have to accept defeat".
