David Axelrod Bio | Wiki

David Axelrod (Full name: David M. Axelrod) is a popular American former White House official, political consultant, and analyst presently working at CNN as the Senior Political Commentator.

Additionally, he also works as the director of the non-partisan at the University of Chicago Institute of Politics. David is popularly recognized for working for Barrack Obama as his chief strategist during his presidential campaigns and was promoted to Senior Advisor of the President after the election.

David Axelrod Campaigns

In 1987 David was hired by Harold Washington, Chicago who was the first black mayor. Soon he became a key player in campaigning for black candidates. David has campaigned for several black candidates including John F. Street in Philadelphia, Michael R. White in Cleveland, Anthony A. Williams in Washington, Dennis Archer in Detroit, and Lee P. Brown in Houston.

In January David was hired by Oregon Governor Neil Goldschmidt as a media consultant in his re-election campaign. However, in February Neil Goldschmidt announced that he would not seek re-election. David was still retained by the Liberal Party of Ontario to help Dalton McGuinty win the elections.

He also worked as a consultant for an Illinois-area utility that operated the largest fleet of nuclear reactors called the Exelon. In 2006, he consulted several campaigns which were successful.
