Astonishing pictures show London after it was bombed almost 30,000 times during the Blitz

We've compiled a selection of pictures of London during the Blitz - the damage, the damage control, the people keeping calm and carrying on

Between September 1940 and May 1941, London was bombed almost 30,000 times. Around 6,000 people were killed in the first month.

The phrase "Keep Calm and Carry On", now plastered over mugs and notebooks and posters and any insipid keepsakes you can find, was a genuine attitude of "spirited defiance" adopted by the Londoners of the day. My own great-grandmother even refused to go down to a shelter - a fact I'm frankly astonished to be here to tell you, given that she lived in south London, a heavily-bombed area of the capital.

Another phrase, often repeated by Winston Churchill, was "Business as usual" - and it wasn't just the firemen whose working lives carried on as if the city were't crumbling around them. Clerks sat at tables on the street when their offices were destroyed. Postmen even sorted through rubble to find letters that could still be delivered.
