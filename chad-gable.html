    Chad Gable Facts

  • He was a Minnesota high school state champion.
  • In the final of the 2012 U.S. Olympic Trials in the 84 kg category, Chad Gable went on to defeat Jordan Holm 2 to 0.
  • He competed in the Greco-Roman style 84 kg event at the 2012 Summer Olympics and defeated Keitani Graham from the Federated States of Micronesia in the qualification round. He was eventually eliminated from the competition in the next round as he lost 3–0 to Pablo Shorey of Cuba.
  • He signed a contract with the professional wrestling promotion WWE in November 2013 and was assigned to the WWE Performance Center in Orlando, Florida. He adopted the ring name “Chad Gable” as a nod to Olympian Dan Gable.
  • On September 5, 2014, Chad Gable made his in-ring debut at an NXT house show in Cocoa Beach, Florida, and defeated Troy McClain.
  • On December 19, 2014, he lost to then debuting Braun Strowman at an NXT live event in Jacksonville, Florida.
  • On January 8, 2015, Chad Gable made his television debut in an episode of NXT and lost to Tyler Breeze.
  • Along with Jason Jordan, he started a storyline at NXT in May 2015 and the storyline included Chad convincing Jordan to form a new partnership, following the dissolution of Jordan’s team with Tye Dillinger.
  • With Jordan, Chad Gable competed in the first round of the Dusty Rhodes Tag Team Classic tournament on September 2, 2015, and went on to defeat the team of Neville and Solomon Crowe.
  • He answered the United States Championship Open Challenge put forward by champion Kevin Owens on the June 20, 2017, episode of SmackDown and was defeated in the attempt.
  • It was announced on the August 22, 2017, episode of SmackDown Live that General Manager Daniel Bryan had signed a returning Shelton Benjamin to team up with Chad Gable. Thus, a new tag team was created. The duo went on to win over The Ascension the following week in their first match as a tag team.
  • Along with Shelton Benjamin, Chad Gable defeated The Hype Bros on the September 12, 2017, episode of SmackDown Live. The duo again defeated The Hype Bros in a rematch at Hell in a Cell.
  • Chad Gable and Shelton Benjamin defeated The New Day and Rusev and Aiden English in a triple threat match on the January 2, 2018, episode of SmackDown Live to earn another shot at the SmackDown Tag Team titles. However, they lost the following week.
  • As part of the 2018 WWE Superstar Shake-Up, Chad Gable was moved to Raw on April 16, 2018, and thus his tag team with Benjamin was disbanded.
  • He was drafted to SmackDown during the 2019 WWE Superstar Shake-up.
