Patrick Sandoval Biography

Patrick Sandoval is one of the most popular and richest Baseball Pitcher who was born on October 18, 1996 in Mission Viejo, California, United States.

Sandoval was drafted by the Houston Astros in the 11th round of the 2015 Major League Baseball draft. He signed for $900,000, forgoing his commitment to USC. After signing, he made his professional debut that season with the Gulf Coast League Astros where he was 0-3 with a 6.08 ERA in six starts. In 2016, he played for the Greeneville Astros where he pitched to a 2-3 record and 5.30 ERA in 13 games (eight starts), and in 2017, he pitched for the Buies Creek Astros, Tri-City ValleyCats, and the Quad Cities River Bandits where he compiled a combined 3-4 record and 4.09 ERA in 14 games (11 starts) between the three teams. Sandoval began the 2018 season with Quad Cities and was named the starting pitcher for the Midwest League All-Star Game. He was promoted to Buies Creek in late June.

On July 26, 2018, Sandoval (along with international pool space cash) was traded to the Los Angeles Angels in exchange for Martín Maldonado. He was assigned to the Inland Empire 66ers and promoted to the Mobile BayBears in August. In 26 games (20 starts) between Quad Cities, Buies Creek, Inland Empire and Mobile, he was 11-1 with a 2.06 ERA, striking out 145 batters in ​122 ⁄3 innings pitched. He returned to Mobile to begin 2019, and was promoted to the Salt Lake Bees in May after compiling a 0-3 record with a 3.60 ERA over twenty innings.
