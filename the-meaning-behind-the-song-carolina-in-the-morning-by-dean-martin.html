The Meaning Behind The Song: Carolina In the Morning by Dean Martin

I have always been captivated by the song “Carolina In the Morning” by Dean Martin. Whenever I hear it, it transports me back to a simpler time, where the beauty of nature and the love between two people were enough to bring joy and contentment.

“Carolina In the Morning” was written by Gus Kahn and Walter Donaldson and was recorded by Dean Martin for his album titled Swingin’ Down Yonder in 1955. The song exudes a sense of nostalgia and paints a picture of a picturesque morning in the charming state of Carolina.

Nothing could be finer than to be in Carolina in the morning

No one could be sweeter than my sweetie when I meet her in the morning

Where the morning glories twine around the door

Whispering pretty stories I long to hear once more

Strolling with my girlie where the dew is pearly early in the morning

Butterflies all flutter up and kiss each buttercup at dawning

If I had Aladdin’s lamp for only a day

I’d make a wish and here’s what I’d say

Nothing could be finer than to be in Carolina in the morning

Where the morning glories twine around the door

Nothing could be finer than to be in Carolina in the AM

Carolina in the morning

These lyrics beautifully describe the enchantment of being in Carolina in the morning. The singer expresses the joy he feels when greeted by his beloved, referring to her as his “sweetie.” The mention of morning glories twining around the door creates an image of a picturesque cottage, adorned with the beauty of nature.

The song evokes a sense of longing for the past, as the lyrics mention “pretty stories I long to hear once more.” This longing for simpler times and familiar stories is something I can deeply relate to. There is a certain magic in revisiting cherished memories and feeling a connection to the past.

“Carolina In the Morning” also captures the innocence and purity of love. The image of strolling with a loved one, where the dew is pearly early in the morning, creates a romantic and tranquil atmosphere. The mention of butterflies fluttering up and kissing each buttercup at dawning further emphasizes the beauty and tenderness of love.

The song concludes with a wish, as the singer expresses his desire to be in Carolina in the morning. This simple wish reflects the longing for a place where true happiness is found.

“Carolina In the Morning” is a timeless piece that reminds us of the beauty in the simplicity of life and the power of love. Dean Martin’s rendition of this song showcases his smooth and captivating voice, allowing the lyrics to come to life.

Additional Information

Released in 1955, “Carolina In the Morning” was featured on Dean Martin’s album Swingin’ Down Yonder. The album was produced by Voyle Gilmore and Lee Gillette and recorded at Capitol Recording Studio in Hollywood.

On this particular recording, drums were played by Ray S. Toland. The song was copyrighted by Capitol Records and released on August 1, 1955.

Interestingly, “Carolina In the Morning” has also been covered by other artists, including Brent Spiner.

In terms of genre, the song falls under the categories of country, pop, and jazz, showcasing its versatility and appeal to a wide audience.

“Carolina In the Morning” remains a beloved classic that continues to evoke feelings of nostalgia, love, and the beauty of nature. It serves as a reminder to appreciate the simple joys in life and the memories that bring us happiness.
