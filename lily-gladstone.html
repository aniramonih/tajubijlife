All About Killers of the Flower Moon Actor Lily Gladstone

Lily Gladstone (age 37, born August 2, 1986) is a remarkable American actress known for her exceptional talent and strong presence in Hollywood. She proudly embraces her Blackfeet and Nimíipuu heritage, infusing her performances with a deep sense of cultural identity and emotional depth.

Known for her exceptional acting skills, Gladstone has made an indelible mark in Hollywood with her acting, for which she won multiple awards, including the Boston Society of Film Critics Awards, International Cinephile Society, Chlotrudis Society for Independent Films, and Central Ohio Film Critics Association.

Lily Gladstone Acting Journey

In 2004, After Enrolling at the University of Montana’s Davidson Honors College, Gladstone became the first Native American recipient of the prestigious Presidential Leadership Scholarship.

In 2018, she earned BFA in acting with a minor in Native American studies, and during university, she participated in campus plays and student films. From 2009 to 2013, Lily joined Living Voices, a renowned theater company where she performed in many theater acts, including “Frybread Queen.”

While pursuing her career as a theater artist, Gladstone began making strides in the film industry. In 2012, she booked her first role in the film “Universal VIP,” marking the beginning of her on-screen journey.

The following year, she appeared in notable films such as “Jimmy P: Psychotherapy Of A Plains Indian” and “Winter in the Blood.”

However, it was in 2016 when Lily’s life took a transformative turn after securing the role of The Rancher in the film “Certain Women.” Her compelling performance in this film propelled her career forward, opening doors to many opportunities.

Subsequently, she continued to impress audiences with her appearances in projects such as “Buster’s Mal Heart,” “Walking Out,” “Room 104,” “Crash Course,” and “Scalped.”

From 2019 to 2022, Lily’s career thrived with compelling roles. She left her indelible mark on television screens in series like “Tuca & Bertie,” “Billions,” and “Room 104.”

Her filmography during this period also includes notable films such as “Reservation Dogs,” “The Unknown Country,” “First Cow,” “Two Eyes,” and “Freeland,” which solidified her reputation as a talented and sought-after actress.
