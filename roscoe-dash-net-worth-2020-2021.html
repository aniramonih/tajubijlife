Early Life & Biography

Roscoe’s official name is Jeffery Lee Johnson Jr and was born on 2nd April 1990. According to him, in school he tried everything to be good but he was always on the wrong. At the age of 12 his brother introduced him to music and that’s how he got hooked up to hip hop. As both were in the same high school, Mill Creek High School, they formed a rap group of four naming it Black Out Boiz (B.O.B). The bad started so well with their singles getting positive reception, but despite all this, his interest was to be a solo rapper, and so he decided to leave the group.

Personal Life

Most of his personal life has been out of public eye as Roscoe is known to be a private person. According to him, even though he was in music, he wasn’t serious about life and used to deal drugs and was very lucky to never spent life in jail. All this changed at the age of 16 when he impregnated his girlfriend and his daughter was born. He also has another daughter though it’s not known whether it’s with the same mother.

In 2018 through his Facebook account, he announced he is officially out of the singles club after marrying his long-time girlfriend. Describing his wife, Roscoe said she is his best friend and one of the most important people in his life.

Career

As indicated earlier, Roscoe started his music career at the age of 12, and after breaking away from the B.O.B group, he made his basement his recording studio making several mixtapes. His effort didn’t go unnoticed as a sponsor who used to sponsor Travis Porter, a rap group came calling. By the time, he used to go by stage name ATL while with BOB and now that he is going solo he searched for a name in comic books and came with Roscoe Dash.

He released his first hit “All The Way Turnt Up” with Travis Porter but shockingly was only credited as being a featured artist. Roscoe moved on and in 2009 signed with Making Moves Inc (MMI) and later Music Line Group. In December 2011, he released EP J.U.I.C.E, a song that also became a hit.
