Early Beginnings and Rise to Fame

Born on a sunny day in May 1977, in Toms River, New Jersey, Kurt Metzger‘s journey from a Jehovah’s Witness minister to a comedy powerhouse is nothing short of fascinating. His early exposure to comedy shows and a rebellious departure from his religious upbringing paved the path for his unique brand of humor.

Career Highlights

Kurt’s comedic voyage took him from the stages of The Stress Factory in New Brunswick to the high-profile platforms of Comedy Central and Inside Amy Schumer. Notably, his work on the sketch Girl, You Don’t Need Makeup earned him an Emmy, placing him firmly on the map as a force in comedy writing.
