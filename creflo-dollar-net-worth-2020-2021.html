Early Life

Creflo Augustus Dollar Jr. was born on January 28, 1962, in College Park, Georgia, to his parents Emma Dollar and Creflo Augustus Dollar, Sr.

He went to Baptist Church School and had earned his Bachelor’s degree in science from West Georgia College, located in Carrollton, Georgia. He decided to venture into business, immediately after he completed his college.

In 1986, Dollar started developing the World Changers Ministries Christian Center. The first worship of his church was held in Kathleen Mitchell Elementary School’s cafeteria in College Park. It was attended by eight people. The church was later renamed to WCCI (World Changers Church International).

World Dome was built with the finance of almost $20 million, and no financing was done by any bank. As of 2007, it made over $70 million in revenue (for 2006) and had almost 30,000 members.

In 2012, his ministries leased The Bronx’s Loews Paradise Theater to start a new church in New York. Creflo Dollar is also an author and speaks at conferences. Most of the writings are based on the gospel of wealth. He has also written about his career in televangelism.
