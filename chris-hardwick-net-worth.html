Chris Hardwick Overview and Wiki

Early Life and Education

Born in Louisville, Kentucky, Chris Hardwick‘s journey began on November 23, 1971. The son of professional bowler Billy Hardwick, Chris grew up in a household where entertainment and sports were part of daily life.

He attended UCLA, majoring in philosophy, a discipline that would later influence his comedic style.

Early Career

Hardwick’s career began in the mid-1990s as a DJ on Los Angeles radio station KROQ-FM. His early foray into entertainment set the stage for a diverse career path. In 1998, he starred in the UPN comedy Guys Like Us, which aired for a season. This period marked the beginning of his transition from radio to television.

Acting and Film Appearances

Hardwick’s acting career includes appearances in Rob Zombie’s horror films House of 1000 Corpses and Halloween II. He also made a small appearance in Terminator 3: Rise of the Machines. In 2010, he was featured in the film The Mother of Invention.

His guest appearances on shows like CSI: Crime Scene Investigation, Married… with Children, Boy Meets World, and Zoey 101 further showcased his acting versatility.

Hosting and Television Career

Hardwick’s hosting career took off with the MTV game show Trashed in 1993 and later Singled Out from 1995 to 1998. He hosted the syndicated dating show Shipmates and the weekly series Wired Science on PBS. In 2009, he became the host of G4’s Web Soup, a spinoff of E!’s The Soup.

The Rise of Nerdist and Podcasting

In 2010, Hardwick started producing the Nerdist podcast, which quickly gained popularity. He signed a contract with BBC America in 2011 to host a panel talk show based on the podcast. Hardwick’s Nerdist Industries, an entertainment space at Meltdown Comics in Los Angeles, became a hub for his nerd media empire.

In 2012, Nerdist Industries was acquired by Legendary Entertainment, with Hardwick as co-president of Legendary’s digital business.

Voice-over Work

Hardwick’s voice-over work is extensive, including roles in The Radio Adventures of Dr. Floyd, Back at the Barnyard, The Batman, Lego Dimensions, and The Legend of Korra.

He voiced Craig the Snake on Sanjay and Craig and the character Vaughn in the Tales from the Borderlands series.

Hosting Acclaimed Aftershows

Hardwick is perhaps best known for hosting Talking Dead, an aftershow for AMC’s The Walking Dead and Fear the Walking Dead.

He also hosted Talking Bad for Breaking Bad and Talking Saul for Better Call Saul. These shows solidified his position as a prominent figure in television hosting.

Stand-up Comedy

As a stand-up comedian, Hardwick performs with Mike Phirman in the musical comedy duo Hard ‘n Phirm. Their half-hour comedy special Comedy Central Presents: Hard ‘n Phirm premiered in 2008. Hardwick’s stand-up special Mandroid aired on Comedy Central in 2012, followed by Funcomfortable in 2016.

Continued Success and Influence

Hardwick’s influence extends beyond traditional media. He has been a contributing writer for Wired magazine and made regular appearances on shows like The Late Late Show with Craig Ferguson and Chelsea Lately.

His podcast The Nerdist Podcast, later renamed ID10T with Chris Hardwick, has broadcast over 1,000 episodes, further cementing his status as a multifaceted entertainer.

Chris Hardwick’s career is a blend of creativity, innovation, and resilience. From his early days as a radio DJ to becoming a renowned host, actor, and podcaster, Hardwick’s journey is a testament to his enduring appeal and talent in the entertainment industry.

Personal Life

Chris’s life off-screen is equally intriguing. He’s been open about his struggles and triumphs, including his journey to sobriety since 2003. His marriage to Lydia Hearst in 2016 added a new chapter to his personal life, one filled with love and companionship.
