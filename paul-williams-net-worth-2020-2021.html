Early life

Born on September 19, 1940, in Omaha, Nebraska, Paul Williams is an American songwriter, singer, composer, and actor. But he is primarily known for his songwriting. His full name is Paul Hamilton Williams Jr. His father Paul Hamilton Williams was an architectural engineer and his mother Bertha Mae was a homemaker. When Paul was only 13 years old, his father died in a car accident.

Due to this fact, his family moved to Long Beach, California, where he lived with his aunt. He completed his early education from Woodrow Wilson Classic High School. He has two brothers, out of which, one is a great NASA rocket scientist and the second one also has worked as a songwriter too.

Career

After the death of his father, Paul lived with his relatives in California. As he had got the extraordinary knacks in music, his talents found their way without much effort. His professional career as a songwriter began when he met Biff Rose in Los Angeles. At that time, they were working on a TV show. They both came together and wrote the song ‘Fill Your Heart’.

They worked together in next few other songs too. Soon, Paul came in touch with songwriter Roger Nicols. The duo penned down many super hit songs together and became one of the most popular songwriters in Hollywood. Other than his collaboration with Roger, Paul also wrote music for several films and music productions. He also teamed up with many other big stars such as Richard Barone, Scissor Sisters, and a few others.

‘We Have Only Just Begun’, ‘Evergreen’, ‘Rainy Days’, and ‘Mondays’ are some of the most popular songs written by Paul. While working as a songwriter, Paul also tried his hands in acting. He appeared in many TV shows and film. However, as an actor, he couldn’t gain as much success as achieved as a songwriter.

Awards & achievements

As a songwriter, paul achieved considerable success. He was nominated for the Oscar Award five times, out of which he managed to bag it once. He has also won Golden Globe Award, 3 Grammy Awards, and the World Sound Track Award. He has also received the honour of being the member of ‘Songwriters Hall of Fame’.
