Natalie Maines of Dixie Chicks Officially Divorced After 2-Year Legal Battle

Natalie Maines Divorce: After a lengthy and contentious legal battle, Natalie Maines, the lead singer of the Dixie Chicks, has finalized her divorce from her estranged husband, Adrian Pasdar.

The legal dispute lasted for two years and encompassed a range of issues, including the validity of their prenuptial agreement and the amount of monthly financial support.

Official Divorce Judgment in December 2019

According to legal documents obtained by The Blast, the divorce judgment was officially entered on December 19, 2019, marking the formal end of their marriage.

A Bitter Legal Battle

The divorce proceedings were marked by intense disagreements between Maines and Pasdar. One of the contentious issues was the monthly financial support Adrian Pasdar requested.

At one point, he asked the court to compel Maines to pay him a monthly sum of $60,000, which included $44,076 for spousal support and an additional $16,427 for child support, totaling $60,503 each month.

Financial Claims and Community Property in California

During the two-year legal battle, Pasdar claimed that Natalie Maines was extremely wealthy, earning approximately $2 million annually from her music career.

He also alleged that she had a net worth of $50 million and owned property worth $4.5 million. Pasdar argued that Maines had been the primary breadwinner during their marriage, and, in California, where they lived, money earned during a marriage is considered “community property.”

Consequently, he contended that Maines should be responsible for covering various expenses, including the mortgage, household expenses, and a vacation home.

Prenuptial Agreement in Question

A significant point of contention revolved around the prenuptial agreement they had signed before their marriage in 2000.

Maines asserted that both parties had willingly signed the prenup, which safeguarded her Dixie Chicks’ earnings as her own. Pasdar, however, challenged the validity of this agreement.

Settlement Agreement and Division of Finances

Despite the prolonged legal dispute, the couple eventually reached a private settlement in October. While the specifics of the settlement remain undisclosed to the public, it presumably involved an equitable division of their finances, along with arrangements for child support.

Moving On

With the divorce judgment now official, both Natalie Maines and Adrian Pasdar are free to pursue new relationships or life paths, closing the chapter on their turbulent legal battle.
