A Brief Introduction to Izzy Stradlin’s Life

Jeffrey Dean Isbell, best known by his stage name Izzy Stradlin, is an American songwriter and musician. Born on April 8, 1962, in Lafayette, Indiana, he is currently 59 years old. Stradlin started playing guitar at a young age and was heavily influenced by the Rolling Stones’ Keith Richards and Chuck Berry. He eventually moved to Los Angeles in 1982 to pursue a career in music.

Relationships and Children

The marriage was short-lived, and they parted ways only a few months later. He has no children of his own, but he has said in interviews that he is fond of his nieces and nephews.

Height and Appearance

Izzy Stradlin stands at a modest 5’9’’ and weighs approximately 72 kgs. He has always been known to keep a low profile, often sporting a hat and sunglasses, making it hard to miss him in a crowd.

Career

Stradlin was one of the founding members of the iconic rock band Guns N’ Roses, along with Axl Rose, Slash, Duff McKagan, and Steven Adler. The band rose to prominence in the late 1980s, and it’s difficult to overstate the cultural impact they had on the music scene at the time. Izzy served as the band’s rhythm guitarist from 1985 to 1991. During that time, he co-wrote many of the band’s most famous tracks, including “Sweet Child O’ Mine,” “Patience,” “Nightrain,” and “Paradise City.”

After leaving Guns N’ Roses, Stradlin pursued a solo career, producing 12 studio albums, and multiple singles. His discography includes works like “117 Degrees”, “Take a Look at That Guy,” “On Down the Road,” “Izzy Stradlin and the Ju-Ju Hounds” and many more. He is known for his unique composition style and his use of blues and rock influences in his music.
