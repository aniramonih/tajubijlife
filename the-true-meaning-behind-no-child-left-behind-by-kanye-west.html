The True Meaning Behind No Child Left Behind By Kanye West

The phrase "No Child Left Behind" is derived from the No Child Left Behind Act, which Education Week explained was an update to the Elementary and Secondary Education Act of 1965. The law was signed by President George W. Bush in 2002 with the goal of keeping all students' academic progress in check, including special needs learners, minority groups, and children from low-income families. It faced criticism after it was passed, and the Obama administration eventually replaced it with the Every Student Succeeds Act, per The Washington Post.

The theme of Kanye West's song's lyrics focuses on religious devotion and reliance on God during tough times. Rapper Vory's verse on "No Child Left Behind" describes this subject, as he sings, "Back again, I used my back against the wall / Never called on y'all, never count on y'all / I'll always count on God."

Within the past year, Kanye and his wife Kim Kardashian separated, which has appeared to be a very emotional journey for the artist, as reported by TheThings. In addition to the process of making it through his mother's passing, the words may reference how Kanye has had to call on God in order to endure this marital breakup. Furthermore, Kanye has four children with Kardashian, named North, Saint, Chicago, and Psalm West, so the song's title also expresses his devotion to raising his children and never leaving them behind.
