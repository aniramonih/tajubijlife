FAQs about Zoë Kravitz

Who are Zoë Kravitz’s parents?

She is the daughter of famous musician Lenny Kravitz and actress Lisa Bonet. She comes from a family with deep roots in the entertainment industry.

What are some of Zoë’s most notable roles?

Zoë has played significant roles in various films and TV shows, including Angel Salvadore in X-Men: First Class, Christina in The Divergent Series, Leta Lestrange in the Fantastic Beasts series, and Catwoman in The Batman.

When did Zoë start her acting career?

Zoë began her acting career in high school with her first film role in No Reservations (2007). She then appeared in movies like The Brave One and Assassination of a High School President.

What is Zoë’s background in music?

Zoë is also a musician. She fronted the band Elevator Fight in 2009 and later formed the R&B/electropop band Lolawolf, which has released several albums and opened for major artists like Miley Cyrus and Lily Allen.

What are some challenges Zoë faced growing up?

Zoë has spoken openly about her struggles with anorexia and bulimia during her teenage years. She also faced challenges related to her mixed-race heritage, feeling like an outsider at times.

Where did Zoë grow up?

Zoë spent her early years in Topanga Canyon with her mother, Lisa Bonet, and later moved to Miami to live with her father, Lenny Kravitz, at the age of 11.

What is Zoë’s educational background?

Zoë attended Miami Country Day School and Rudolf Steiner School in Manhattan. She began studying at the State University of New York at Purchase but left after a year to pursue her acting career full-time.
