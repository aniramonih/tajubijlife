Dairy Queen adds new Blizzard flavor to its 'royal' collection but theres a catch

DAIRY Queen has launched a new Blizzard – but it won't be around forever.

The sweet treat is a part of the fast food chain's so-called royal offerings, which Dairy Queen describes as being iconic for their "filled center.

The new Blizzard flavor is known as the royal Reese's fluffernutter Blizzard.

It includes marshmallows, chocolate, and peanut butter topping.

"At the center of the Royal REESE'S Fluffernutter Blizzard® Treat — surrounded by our world-famous soft serve mixed with REESE'S Peanut Butter Cups and peanut butter topping — you’ll find sweet, creamy marshmallow," Dairy Queen said in a description of the product.

While prices will vary by location, the cost ranges from $4.46 to $6.50, according to a local store viewed by The U.S. Sun.

The new Reese's Blizzard can be ordered in mini, small, medium, and large sizes.

In total, the Blizzard contains a whopping 149 grams of total sugar if ordered in a large.

The item is available for a limited time only at participating Dairy Queen stores.

MORE NEWS AT DAIRY QUEEN

The new item comes as the ice cream chain has brought back the pumpkin pie Blizzard for the fall.

It has been around seasonally for 32 years.

The fall treat contains pumpkin pie pieces, and vanilla soft serve that's topped with whipped cream and nutmeg.

Also, Dairy Queen has brought back its caramel fudge cheesecake Blizzard.

The treat features soft serve ice cream that's mixed with cheesecake and salted caramel pieces, as well as decadent fudge.

"This indulgent treat is the perfect end to a summer day, meant to be enjoyed on a warm night as you watch a summer sunset blaze across the sky," Dairy Queen said in a description of the caramel fudge cheesecake Blizzard.

FALL FESTIVALS ELSEWHERE

But Dairy Queen is far from the only brand that has been launching fall items this summer.

Starbucks last week launched its seasonal lineup.

It includes returning favorites such as the pumpkin spice latte, pumpkin cream cold brew, apple crisp oat milk macchiato, pumpkin cream cheese muffin, owl cake pop, and pumpkin and pepita loaf.

There are also three new items on the fall menu this year, which are as follows:

  • Iced pumpkin cream chai tea latte
  • Baked apple croissant
  • Iced apple crisp oat milk shaken espresso

Krispy Kreme has unveiled its full lineup, containing various items from coffees to doughnuts.

This includes bringing back the pumpkin spice original glazed and pumpkin spice cake doughnut.

Also, two new donuts including the pumpkin spice cheesecake swirl and pumpkin spice maple pecan are joining the menu.

And even grocery chains have been getting festive.

Aldi has introduced the organic apple cinnamon and pumpkin spice coconut clusters.

They sell in 10-ounce bags for $6.29 apiece.
