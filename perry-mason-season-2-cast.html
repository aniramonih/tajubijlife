Chris Chalk as Paul Drake

Paul Drake, played by Chris Chalk is a detective-minded beat cop. Because of his street smarts and investigative prowess, he is often the one to find crucial evidence that leads to arrests and convictions.

Contrasting Mason’s more serious demeanor, Drake’s portrayal as having a lively disposition and a decent sense of humor brings humor, warmth, and a feeling of adventure to the show.

Paul faces a city and police force that are both highly racialized and oppressive throughout the show. Before moving on to acting in movies and television shows, Chalk began his career as a stage actor appearing in productions in New York and across the country. His film T.V. edited list includes Gotham and 12 Years an enslaved person.

Eric Lange as Gene Holcomb

Gene Holcomb played by Eric Lange is a crooked cop who will go to any lengths to achieve his goals, including breaking the law. Holcomb is a nuanced and intriguing protagonist who raises the stakes and complexity of the show. His drive makes him an engaging character and a suitable opponent for Mason.

Lange was cast as Stuart Radzinsky, a recurring character on Lost, an ABC series, in 2009. After that, from 2010 to 2013, he portrayed Erwin Sikowitz on the Nickelodeon show Victorious. In 2013, however, he broke through to a broader audience by performing  David Tate/Kenneth Hasting on the FX series The Bridge.

Diarra Kilpatrick as Clara Drake

Mason’s private eye, Paul Drake has a wife named Clara, played by Diarra Kilpatrick. Clara is a likable protagonist who is making positive changes in her life. The series delves deeply into Clara and Paul’s relationship. Paul is caught between his allegiance to Mason and his love for his wife, making their relationship difficult and tense.

Kilpatrick has been in several episodes of the ABC Digital series American Koko which adds to his impressive acting resume. Kilpatrick has also appeared in parts of Private Practice, The Last O.G. and The Twilight Zone.
